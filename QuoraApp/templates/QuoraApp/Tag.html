Tanaya Wankar asked 3 Jan 2020
What is the difference between tier 1 and tier 3 college students in India?
Prathamesh Thombre answered 4 Jan 2020

The ground reality is colleges differ in various aspects. Some are “tier 1” and some “tier 3”. Let’s take a look at some of the glaring differences between the students of these colleges : Connections - This is the most obvious one. Students need better alumni to get more opportunities. In tier 1 colleges, this is something which is really stressed upon. Their alumni are at many great positions, and they come in handy whenever needed. Health and wellness features availability - This is not so stressed upon in Indian context, but many colleges do not have sports fields and other facilities. These contribute to well balanced development of students. Mentality - Many students develop a inferiority complex or a superiority complex depending on the college they are in. Both of them are equally toxic in the long run. But it gives you a certain level of confidence when you are from a tier 1 college.
